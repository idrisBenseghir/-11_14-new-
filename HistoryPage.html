History

The birth of 'global warming'

In a report called "Restoring the Quality of Our Environment", scientists proposed that increasing temperatures in the atmosphere were caused by a buildup of carbon dioxide. It wasn’t until 1975 that the term “global warming” was coined by geoscientist Wallace Broecker. The issue took years to reach mainstream understanding.

Earth Day

The organizers wanted to raise awareness of concerns such as pollution and toxic waste. Inspired by the anti-war movement led by students, Earth Day has become a global event, with over 200 million participants in 141 countries in 1990.
