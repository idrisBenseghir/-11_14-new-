History

The Birth of 'Global Warming'

In 1965, scientists proposed that increasing atmospheric temperatures were caused by a buildup of carbon dioxide. The term “global warming” was coined by Wallace Broecker in 1975.

Location: United States

Year: 1965

Earth Day

Started in 1970 in the U.S., Earth Day aimed to raise awareness about pollution and toxic waste. It became global in 1990 with participation from 141 countries.

Location: United States

Date: April 22, 1970

Heatwaves

By the late 1980s, droughts and record heat led to global media coverage and were linked to "global warming," notably by British scientists in 1989.

Location: United States

Year: 1980

The IPCC

In 1988, the UN launched the IPCC to assess climate science and provide reports used in international climate negotiations.

Organization: United Nations

Year: 1988

The Rio Earth Summit

The 1992 Earth Summit set principles for environmental protection and sustainable development, adopted by 178 countries.

Organization: United Nations

Year: 1992

The Kyoto Protocol

In 1997, countries agreed to reduce emissions by an average of 5% during 2008-2012, though the U.S. did not ratify it.

Location: Kyoto, Japan

Year: 1997

Demonstrations

Global climate demonstrations began in 2005, notably during the UN climate talks in Montreal, and continue annually.

Organization: United Nations

Year: 2000

Student Pressure

From 2011 onwards, students worldwide have advocated for universities to divest from fossil fuels, with increasing success.

Location: United States, United Kingdom

Years: 2011 - Present
